On 21 May 1917 the Imperial War Graves Commission was founded.
Born in Clifton, Bristol, he graduated from the University of Paris in 1894.
When the First World War started in August 1914, Ware attempted to join the British Army but was rejected because he was too old.
When war broke out, he continued to serve as vice-chairman of the IWGC and was re-appointed director-general of Graves Registration and Enquiries.
Two years later, Ware became editor of The Morning Post and returned to England.
